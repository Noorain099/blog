Empowering Women, Inspiring Change: Welcome to IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE)


In a fast-evolving world, the need for inclusivity and diversity in technology is more vital than ever. At IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE), Muffakham Jah College of Engineering and Technology (MJCET), we are dedicated to empowering women to rise in engineering, technology, and beyond!

IEEE WIE MJCET is committed to:

Inspiring the next generation of women engineers by creating a supportive space to learn and innovate. Educating students through workshops, talks, and hands-on sessions led by experts in the field. Empowering women by building confidence and providing tools to succeed in the tech industry. We believe that every woman deserves the chance to excel in engineering, and we are here to make that vision a reality.

Why Ethical Hacking?

Ethical hacking is more than just finding vulnerabilities in systems; it's about making the digital world a safer place. Ethical hackers use their skills to identify security flaws and help prevent malicious attacks. As technology advances, so do the tactics of cybercriminals, and ethical hackers are on the frontlines, protecting data and safeguarding privacy.
